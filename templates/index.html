    • Privacy Notice
  • As part of this research a study will be conducted in which non-personally identifiable data will be collected from a group of individuals who have dyslexia for the purposes of academic research.

  • This study will present several authentication methods through online means to people with dyslexia in order to ascertain which might be preferable or more accessible to these users. We are interested in your advice and feedback in relation to the methodology we are going to follow in this study. We want to make sure this is adequate for people with dyslexia, therefore, we are going to organize co-creation sessions to build the methodology and the website for this study.

  • This study will form part of a research project being conducted by the research students outlined above under the supervision of Dr. Irina Tal and will explore accessible authentication for users with dyslexia.

  • The data controller for this project is DCU.

  • The DCU Data Protection Officer is Mr. Martin Ward (data.protection@dcu.ie Ph.: 7005118 / 7008257).

  • The non-personally identifiable data collected as part of this study will be stored securely on a DCU google drive in compliance with DCU’s data retention policy and will only be accessible strictly by the research students and principal investigator outlined above.

  • The data will be retained until November 2022 after which it will be fully erased in line with DCU’s data retention policy.

  • Participants are free to contact any of the people involved in the research to seek further clarification and information.

  • Participants in this study should be aware that they have the right to lodge a complaint with the

  • Participants should be aware that confidentiality of information can only be protected within the limitations of the law - i.e., it is possible for data to be subject to subpoena, freedom of information claim or mandated reporting by some professions.

  • Participants should be aware that they can withdraw from this study at any point, in which case no future data collection will take place. Any data collected up to the point at which consent is withdrawn will be erased if requested.


    • Data Collected:
  • There will be some demographic data collected on a voluntary basis (there will be an option to not answer in each category).

  • Data will be collected on the usability/preferability of the authentication methods presented and metrics will be stored such as the time taken for each authentication method by each participant.
